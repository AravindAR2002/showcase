Strategic Sourcing & Project Execution

Achieved 21% cost savings and secured key industry memberships to drive market expansion.

The Business Challenge

As Evershine prepared for a major expansion, it faced several operational challenges: a need to upgrade facilities cost-effectively, a non-optimized vendor network with uncompetitive pricing, and a lack of key industry accreditations required to bid for larger government tenders and enhance market credibility.

My Key Initiatives & Impact

As a member of the Founder's Office, I was tasked with driving three critical initiatives to address these challenges:

1. Project Management: Experience Centre Execution

I took end-to-end ownership of a critical facility upgrade. My process involved obtaining architectural plans, sourcing all materials via platforms like IndiaMart, and managing a cross-functional team of suppliers, fabricators, and contractors.

Outcome: Spearheaded the project from plan to completion, delivering the Experience Centre on time and achieving 21% cost savings compared to the initial contractor quotation through effective vendor negotiation.

2. Strategic Sourcing & Vendor Negotiation

I proactively engaged with over 20 private vendors and 5 government departments to build a robust partner network. This involved identifying underperforming contracts, such as with an IT vendor supplying goods at uncompetitive prices.

Outcome: Drove significant cost savings across multiple categories, including securing a 20% reduction in all IT procurement costs by onboarding a new, more competitive vendor. This established a network of reliable partners for critical needs like ISO certification and product testing.

3. Securing Strategic Memberships: CIDC & PEC

To boost the company's brand and eligibility for new opportunities, I led the process to gain membership with key industry councils. This involved coordinating with internal departments to gather financial data, preparing all documentation, and leading negotiations with council representatives.

Outcome: Successfully secured full memberships with both the Construction Industry Development Council (CIDC) and the Plastics Export Promotion Council (PEC). These affiliations immediately enhance the company's eligibility for government tenders and export opportunities.
