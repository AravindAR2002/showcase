Unit Cost & Pricing Analysis

A data-driven analysis that enabled a strategic 5% price increase.

The Business Problem

Four months after launching a new rebar manufacturing facility, the product line's revenues were underperforming against initial projections. Management suspected a discrepancy between projected and actual production costs but lacked the granular data to confirm this, preventing them from making informed pricing adjustments. I was tasked with determining the true unit cost to form the basis of a revised pricing strategy.

My Approach & Methodology

I took the lead on this financial investigation by gathering and consolidating five months of comprehensive production, consumption, and expense data from the factory and Tally software. Applying a proportional cost allocation method, I meticulously analyzed the data to determine the precise per-kilogram and per-meter manufacturing cost for each individual rebar size.

The Key Insight

The analysis revealed a critical finding: the actual, all-inclusive manufacturing cost was 5% higher than the initial projections used for the launch pricing. This discrepancy was the primary driver of the revenue shortfall, as the company was unintentionally underpricing its product.

Outcome & Impact

  • Identified Critical Cost Variance: My work provided the first accurate, data-backed evidence of the 5% cost overrun, replacing assumptions with facts.
  • Enabled Strategic Price Correction: Armed with this precise data, I presented my findings to management, giving them the confidence to implement an immediate 5% increase in the product's selling price.
  • Improved Profitability: This data-driven price adjustment directly addressed the revenue problem, aligning the product's pricing with its true manufacturing cost and setting it on a clear path to improved profitability.
